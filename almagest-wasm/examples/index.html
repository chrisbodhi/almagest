Understanding Space Tether Physics

Space tethers represent a revolutionary approach to propellantless propulsion, using orbital mechanics and advanced materials to transfer momentum without chemical rockets.

üöÄ Momentum Exchange Tethers

Momentum exchange tethers are rotating space structures that can transfer momentum between different orbital altitudes without expending propellant. They work by exploiting the fact that objects at different altitudes have different orbital velocities.

When a payload is caught at the lower tip of a rotating tether and released at the upper tip, it gains velocity and altitude. The tether system loses a corresponding amount of momentum, which can be restored using attitude control systems or by catching payloads moving in the opposite direction.

üß¨ Material Science Critical

The performance of space tethers is fundamentally limited by material properties. The characteristic velocity determines the maximum tip speed achievable before centrifugal forces exceed the material's tensile strength.

üåç Orbital Mechanics Principles

Tether systems operate in the complex gravitational environment of Earth orbit, where altitude differences create velocity gradients. The tether must be precisely controlled to maintain stability while providing useful momentum transfer.

Key parameters include orbital altitude, tether length, rotation rate, and the mass distribution along the tether. These determine the system's efficiency, operational envelope, and maximum payload capacity.
